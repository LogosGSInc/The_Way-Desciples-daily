# Y\#\# **The Way: Disciple’s Daily**

**Date:** 22 Nisan 5786 | April 10, 2026
**Torah Portion:** *Acharei Mot* (After the Death)
**Reading:** 1st Aliyah (Leviticus 16:1–16:17)

### **Today’s Torah Reading**

**Leviticus 16:1–3**
> "The Lord spoke to Moses after the death of the two sons of Aaron, when they drew near before the Lord and died, and the Lord said to Moses, 'Tell Aaron your brother not to come at any time into the Holy Place inside the veil, before the mercy seat that is on the ark, so that he may not die. For I will appear in the cloud over the mercy seat. But in this way Aaron shall come into the Holy Place: with a bull from the herd for a sin offering and a ram for a burnt offering.'"
>

### **Scriptural Commentary: The Weight of Holiness**

The opening of *Parashat Acharei Mot* serves as a sobering reminder that our God is a **consuming fire** (Deuteronomy 4:24). This portion is inextricably linked to the tragedy of Nadab and Abihu, who offered "strange fire" and perished. The instruction here isn't just about ritual; it is about the **sovereignty of God’s presence**.
**The Blueprint of Redemption**
The high priest could not enter the Holy of Holies on his own terms. He required a blood sacrifice and a cloud of incense. This shadows the reality described in **Hebrews 9:11-12**:
> *"But when Christ appeared as a high priest of the good things that have come... he entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption."*
>
**The Veil and the Mercy Seat**
The "Mercy Seat" (*Kapporet*) was the place where the blood was sprinkled to make atonement. In the Tanakh, **Psalm 85:10** tells us that *"Steadfast love and faithfulness meet; righteousness and peace kiss each other."* At the Mercy Seat, the justice of God (which demands death for sin) and the mercy of God (which desires life for the sinner) meet perfectly.
Because of the work of the Greater High Priest, the veil that Aaron had to fear is now torn (**Matthew 27:51**). We are commanded in **Hebrews 10:19-22** to "draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith," yet we must never lose the "godly fear and awe" (**Hebrews 12:28**) that this Torah portion demands.

### **The Great Commission \& Daily Walk**

We are called to "make disciples of all nations" (**Matthew 28:19**). A disciple is one who understands the cost of their access to God. We do not preach a "cheap grace" that ignores the holiness of the Father, but a "costly grace" purchased by the blood of the Lamb. To walk in integrity today is to honor the boundaries God has set while rejoicing in the access He has provided.
**Practical Application for Today:**
Identify one area of your life where you have become "casual" or "familiar" with God’s holiness (your speech, your secret thoughts, or your integrity in business). Today, intentionally **consecrate** that area. Before you begin your work, pause and offer a prayer of "incense"—gratitude and reverence—acknowledging that you stand in His presence only by His mercy.
*Go forth and make disciples, walking in the Way of the King.*
